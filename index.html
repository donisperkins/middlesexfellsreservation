What Is Middlesex Fells Reservation?

Middlesex Fells Reservation — often called simply "the Fells" — is a public recreation area covering more than 2,200 acres in Malden, Medford, Melrose, Stoneham, and Winchester, Massachusetts. Established in 1894, it is one of the oldest and largest urban nature preserves in the United States, located just 5 miles north of Boston. The park is managed by the Massachusetts Department of Conservation and Recreation (DCR) and is part of the Metropolitan Park System of Greater Boston. With over 100 miles of mixed-use trails, visitors can hike, bike, paddle, and explore for all difficulty levels — all for free.

What to Do at Middlesex Fells

From rugged ridge hikes with Boston skyline views to lazy afternoons by the water, the Fells packs a surprising amount of adventure into one of Greater Boston's most beloved green spaces.

Hiking

The reservation's 2,575 acres offer suitable terrain for hikers of all experience levels, from easy pond-side walks to challenging ridge scrambles over exposed granite. Download the trail map before you go — some sections aren't well marked.

Explore Trails

Mountain Biking

The Mountain Bike Loop covers 6.5 miles on fire roads with some single-track sections, offering a scenic ride through the western Fells with an average riding time of about one hour.

Mountain Biking Guide

Canoe & Kayaking

Rent a kayak or canoe and explore Spot Pond, right in the heart of Middlesex Fells. It's one of the most popular warm-weather destinations in the park.

Canoe & Kayaking Guide

Dogs & Off-Leash Area

Dogs are welcome throughout the reservation on leash, with a designated off-leash area at the Sheepfold field where dogs must remain under voice control. Up to four dogs per handler allowed.

Dogs Guide

Rock Climbing

The Fells' granite outcrops draw climbers looking for a quick technical challenge close to the city. No permit required for bouldering.

Picnicking

The Sheepfold is 10 acres of open field, ideal for picnics, frisbee, and informal games. It's the most popular family gathering spot in the park.

Birdwatching & Wildlife

Keep an eye out for coyote, red fox, and white-tailed deer throughout the reservation. The reservoirs and forested ridges make this one of the best urban birding spots in eastern Massachusetts.

Winter Activities

When the snow falls, the Fells transforms into a cross-country skiing and snowshoeing destination — same trails, zero crowds.

Cross-Country Skiing

Fishing

Bass, perch, and pickerel at Spot Pond, Bellevue Pond, and Wright's Pond. MA fishing license required.

Fishing Guide

Horseback Riding

Bridle paths wind through the West Fells for riders bringing their own horse — no rentals on site.

Ponds & Landmarks

Water and granite define the Fells. Here's where to find the reservation's best-known spots.

Spot Pond

The largest pond in the Fells at ~340 acres — kayaking, canoeing, and fishing in the East Fells.

Elizur Wright Tower

The historic stone tower atop Bear Hill, 317 feet up, with 360-degree views to the Boston skyline.

Pickerel Rock

A dramatic granite outcrop in the East Fells overlooking Spot Pond — a locals' favorite lookout.

Bellevue Pond

A quiet West Fells pond for fishing and peaceful walks, away from the busier trailheads.

Wright's Pond

Tucked into the West Fells — one of the most off-the-beaten-path spots in the reservation.

Middlesex Fells Reservoir

The historic water-supply reservoir at the heart of the park — scenic perimeter trails, restricted water access.

How to Get to Middlesex Fells

The Fells is easy to reach by car, public transit, or on foot from several surrounding neighborhoods.

By Car

Free parking is available at multiple lots throughout the reservation. Major lots include Sheepfold, Flynn Rink, and South Border Road trailheads. If the reservation parking lot is full, do not park on Governors Ave — it's permit parking only. Look for parking on nearby side streets.

Parking Guide

By Public Transit

The Oak Grove T stop on the Orange Line is a 15-minute walk from the Fells. Three bus routes also connect to the park, including MBTA Bus #99 from Malden Center and Bus #132 from Malden Center to Stone Zoo.

Visitor Center

The Botume House Visitor Center is located at 4 Woodland Road, Stoneham, on the eastern shore of Spot Pond, half a mile south of Stone Zoo. Trail maps and DCR program info are available there.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Middlesex Fells Reservation free?

Yes. Middlesex Fells Reservation is free and open to the public year-round. There is no entrance fee, and parking is also free at all designated lots.

Where is Middlesex Fells Reservation?

Middlesex Fells Reservation spans five towns in Middlesex County: Malden, Medford, Melrose, Stoneham, and Winchester, Massachusetts — about 5 miles north of downtown Boston.

How big is Middlesex Fells Reservation?

Middlesex Fells Reservation covers 2,575 acres of rocky, hilly terrain in eastern Massachusetts, making it one of the largest urban nature reserves in the northeastern United States.

Are dogs allowed at Middlesex Fells?

Dogs are welcome throughout the reservation on leash, except inside the designated off-leash area at the Sheepfold field, where dogs must remain under voice control.

Can you swim at Middlesex Fells?

No. Swimming is prohibited throughout the Middlesex Fells Reservation — including Spot Pond, Bellevue Pond, Wright's Pond, and the High, Middle, and Low Reservoirs in Winchester. This applies to all water bodies in the reservation.

How many trails does Middlesex Fells have?

The reservation has over 100 miles of trails for hiking, mountain biking, cross-country skiing, and horseback riding, ranging from easy nature walks to challenging ridge routes.

What is the best trail at Middlesex Fells?

The Skyline Trail is the most popular. It offers panoramic Boston views from the park's granite ridges and covers 7.6 miles. For beginners, the Long Pond Nature Trail at 1.7 miles is a great starting point.

Is Middlesex Fells good for families?

Yes — it's one of the best family outdoor destinations near Boston. The Sheepfold picnic area, easy flat trails, and open fields make it ideal for kids of all ages.

What towns is Middlesex Fells in?

Middlesex Fells Reservation covers land in Malden, Medford, Melrose, Stoneham, and Winchester, Massachusetts.

Is there public transit to Middlesex Fells?

Yes — the Oak Grove T stop on the Orange Line is a 15-minute walk from the reservation. MBTA bus routes #99 and #132 also provide access from Malden Center.
