# Sheepfold Dog Park at Middlesex Fells Reservation

The Sheepfold Dog Park at Middlesex Fells Reservation is one of the largest off-leash dog parks in Massachusetts, and a favorite destination for dog owners in Medford, Stoneham, Winchester, and surrounding towns. Open year-round with separate areas for large and small dogs, it's one of the best dog parks in Medford, MA and the greater Boston area.

## About the Sheepfold Dog Park

The Sheepfold is a large fenced off-leash area located in the northeastern section of Middlesex Fells Reservation off Pond Street in Stoneham. The park features wide open fields, wooded edges, and plenty of room for dogs to run. It's maintained by the Department of Conservation and Recreation (DCR) and is free to use.

### Large Dog Area

The large dog area is a spacious fenced field ideal for active dogs who love to run and socialize. It's the larger of the two areas and typically the busier section on weekends.

### Small Dog Area

A separate fenced section is available for small or timid dogs, providing a safer space away from larger breeds. Both areas have water access during warmer months.

## Rules & Regulations

-   Dogs must be licensed and up to date on vaccinations
-   Owners must have a leash on hand at all times, even in off-leash areas
-   Clean up after your dog — bags are available on site
-   Aggressive dogs must be removed immediately
-   Maximum 3 dogs per handler
-   No puppies under 4 months

## Parking & Getting There

The primary access point is the [Upper Sheepfold parking lot](/parking) off Pond Street in Stoneham. This lot fills quickly on weekend mornings. Arrive early or check the [South Border Road lot](/parking) as an alternative and walk in via trail. See our full [parking guide](/parking) for details.

## Best Times to Visit

Weekday mornings are the least crowded and ideal for dogs who prefer a calmer environment. Weekend afternoons are the busiest. The park is open year-round including winter, though the fields can get muddy during spring thaw.
