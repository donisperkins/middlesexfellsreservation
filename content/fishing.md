# Fishing at Middlesex Fells Reservation

Middlesex Fells Reservation offers excellent freshwater fishing at several ponds and reservoirs within its 2,200 acres, just minutes from Boston. Whether you're a seasoned angler or a first-time fisherman, the Fells provides a peaceful, accessible spot to cast a line in a natural setting.

## Fishing Spots

### Spot Pond

[Spot Pond](/spot-pond) is the most popular fishing destination in the reservation. This 100-acre pond in Stoneham is stocked and known for largemouth bass, yellow perch, and chain pickerel. Bank fishing access is available at multiple points around the pond's edge.

### Flynn Pond

Flynn Pond is a smaller, quieter fishing spot in the western section of the reservation, accessible via trail from the South Border Road area. Less crowded than Spot Pond and a good option on busy weekends.

### Quarter Mile Pond & Doleful Pond

Additional ponds within the reservation offer bank fishing access and are worth exploring for anglers looking to find less-trafficked spots.

## Fish Species

Common species found in Middlesex Fells waters include:

-   Largemouth bass
-   Yellow perch
-   Chain pickerel
-   Bluegill
-   Brown bullhead

## Fishing License Requirements

A valid Massachusetts freshwater fishing license is required for all anglers 15 years of age and older. Licenses are available online through the Massachusetts Division of Fisheries and Wildlife or at many local sporting goods stores.

## Rules & Regulations

-   Fishing is permitted from the bank only in most areas — no motorized boats
-   Follow all Massachusetts DFW size and catch limits
-   No fishing from dams or restricted water supply areas
-   Pack out all fishing line and trash

## Getting There & Parking

The closest parking to Spot Pond is along Spot Pond Brook Parkway in Stoneham. For the western ponds, the [South Border Road lot](/parking) is the best starting point. See our full [Parking & Directions](/parking) guide for details.
