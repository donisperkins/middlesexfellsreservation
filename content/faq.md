# Frequently Asked Questions — Middlesex Fells Reservation

## Is there an admission fee for Middlesex Fells Reservation?

No. Middlesex Fells Reservation is free and open to the public year-round. There is no entry fee for trails, parking, or most facilities.

## What are the park hours?

The reservation is open dawn to dusk, seven days a week, year-round. Parking lots are generally open sunrise to sunset. See our [Hours & Fees](/hours) page for details.

## Where do I park?

There are several parking lots throughout the reservation. The most popular is the South Border Road lot in Medford. The Upper Sheepfold lot off Pond Street in Stoneham serves the dog park area. See our full [Parking & Directions](/parking) guide for all lot locations and weekend tips.

## Are dogs allowed at Middlesex Fells?

Yes. Dogs are welcome throughout the reservation on a leash. The [Sheepfold dog park](/dog-park) offers a designated off-leash area with separate sections for large and small dogs. It is one of the largest off-leash dog areas in Massachusetts.

## Can I go swimming at Middlesex Fells?

No. Swimming is prohibited throughout the Middlesex Fells Reservation, including Spot Pond, Bellevue Pond, Wright's Pond, and all reservoirs. This rule is enforced by the DCR year-round. See our [Swimming](/swimming) page for more information and nearby alternatives.

## Can I fish at Middlesex Fells?

Yes. Fishing is permitted at Spot Pond and other water bodies within the reservation. A valid Massachusetts fishing license is required for anglers 15 and older. See our [Fishing](/fishing) page for more details.

## Is mountain biking allowed?

Yes. Mountain biking is permitted on designated trails throughout the reservation. See our [Mountain Biking](/mountain-biking) page for trail information.

## What trails are at Middlesex Fells?

The reservation has over 100 miles of trails ranging from easy walks to challenging ridge hikes. The most popular are the [Skyline Trail](/skyline-trail) and the [Rock Circuit Trail](/rock-circuit-trail). Visit our [Trail Map](/trail-map) for a full overview.

## Is there a trail map available?

Yes. Visit our [Trail Map](/trail-map) page to view and download the official Middlesex Fells trail map.

## Is Middlesex Fells open in winter?

Yes. The reservation is open year-round. Trails are available for hiking and [cross-country skiing](/cross-country-skiing) when snow conditions allow.

## Who manages Middlesex Fells Reservation?

Middlesex Fells Reservation is managed by the Massachusetts Department of Conservation and Recreation (DCR).

## Is this the official Middlesex Fells website?

No. This is an independent informational website. For official information, visit the Massachusetts DCR website.
