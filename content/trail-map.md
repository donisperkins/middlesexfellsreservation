# Trail Map of Middlesex Fells Reservation

Middlesex Fells Reservation has over 100 miles of trails spread across 2,200 acres in five towns. The trail map below covers all hiking, biking, and multi-use trails throughout the western and eastern sections of the reservation.

## About the Trail System

Trails at Middlesex Fells are color-coded and blazed with painted markers on trees and rocks. The main trails are:

-   **White blazes** — [Skyline Trail](/skyline-trail) (6.7 miles, challenging)
-   **Yellow blazes** — [Rock Circuit Trail](/rock-circuit-trail) (4.6 miles, moderate to challenging)
-   **Additional trails** — A network of unmarked and lightly marked paths connects throughout the reservation

The western section (Medford/Stoneham/Winchester side) contains the most rugged terrain and is where most hikers and bikers spend their time. The eastern section (Malden/Stoneham side) is generally flatter and includes [Spot Pond](/spot-pond) and the [Sheepfold dog park](/dog-park).

## Download the Official Trail Map

The official Middlesex Fells Reservation trail map is produced by the Massachusetts Department of Conservation and Recreation (DCR). It shows all trails, parking lots, ponds, and key landmarks including [Elizur Wright Tower](/observation-tower).

[Download the official trail map from the Massachusetts DCR website](https://www.mass.gov/locations/middlesex-fells-reservation)

## Key Landmarks on the Map

-   **Bear Hill / Elizur Wright Tower** — highest point in the reservation, 360-degree views
-   **Spot Pond** — largest pond, eastern section, kayaking and fishing
-   **Sheepfold** — off-leash dog park, northeastern section
-   **South Border Road Trailhead** — main access point, western section
