# Hiking at Middlesex Fells Reservation

Middlesex Fells Reservation is one of the best hiking destinations near Boston, with over 100 miles of trails winding through 2,200 acres of rocky forest, scenic ponds, and elevated ridgelines. Whether you're looking for a quick walk or a full-day challenge, the Fells has a trail for every level.

## Trail Overview

Hiking trails at Middlesex Fells range from flat, easy paths around the reservation's ponds to rugged ridge climbs with significant elevation gain. Most trails are natural surface — expect rocks, roots, and uneven terrain throughout. Sturdy footwear is recommended for all but the most casual walks.

## Best Hiking Trails at Middlesex Fells

### Skyline Trail

The [Skyline Trail](/skyline-trail) is the signature hike of the Fells — a 6.7-mile loop over rocky ridges and forested summits with panoramic views. Marked with white blazes. Difficulty: Challenging.

### Rock Circuit Trail

The [Rock Circuit Trail](/rock-circuit-trail) is a 4.6-mile loop through the rocky heart of the western Fells, passing near Bear Hill and [Elizur Wright Tower](/observation-tower). Marked with yellow blazes. Difficulty: Moderate to challenging.

## Hiking to Elizur Wright Tower

One of the most popular hiking destinations in the reservation is [Elizur Wright Tower](/observation-tower) at Bear Hill summit, offering 360-degree views including the Boston skyline on clear days. Both the Skyline Trail and Rock Circuit Trail provide routes to the tower.

## Trail Rules & Etiquette

-   Dogs must be on a leash on all hiking trails
-   Stay on marked trails to protect vegetation and prevent erosion
-   Pack out all trash
-   Yield to uphill hikers on steep sections
-   Horses have right of way on multi-use trails

## Getting There & Parking

The most popular trailhead is the [South Border Road parking lot](/parking) in Medford. The Bear Hill lot in Stoneham is a good alternative when South Border Road is full. See our full [Parking & Directions](/parking) guide for locations and weekend tips.
