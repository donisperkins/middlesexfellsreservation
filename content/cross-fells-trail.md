# Cross Fells Trail at Middlesex Fells Reservation

The Cross Fells Trail is one of the best ways to experience the full breadth of Middlesex Fells Reservation. Running east to west across the park, this 4.7-mile trail takes you through some of the Fells' most rugged and least-trafficked terrain — rocky ridges, quiet forest, and a handful of spots where you can stop and feel genuinely away from it all, even though you're a few miles outside Boston.

It's not a beginner trail. The Cross Fells earns its "difficult" rating, with uneven footing, elevation changes, and stretches that require your full attention. But if you're up for it, the payoff is worth every step.

* * *

## Trail Overview

-   **Distance:** 4.7 miles (one way)
-   **Difficulty:** Difficult
-   **Trail Markers:** Blue blazes
-   **Trail Type:** Point-to-point
-   **Location:** Middlesex Fells Reservation, Eastern Massachusetts

The Cross Fells Trail runs roughly east to west through the reservation, connecting trailheads on opposite sides of the park. It passes through both the East and West Fells sections, linking up with several other trails along the way — including the [Skyline Trail](/skyline-trail/) and the [Rock Circuit Trail](/rock-circuit-trail/).

* * *

## Distance, Difficulty & Trail Details

At 4.7 miles one way, the Cross Fells Trail is best approached as either a point-to-point hike (with a car shuttle or ride share) or as part of a larger loop using connecting trails.

The trail is rated **difficult** due to:

-   Rocky, uneven terrain throughout
-   Multiple elevation changes with no single major summit
-   Sections with minimal trail maintenance — roots, loose rock, and mud are common
-   Limited shade in some exposed ridge sections

**Best for:** Experienced hikers comfortable with rocky footing. Not recommended for young children, strollers, or those with limited mobility.

**Dogs:** Dogs are welcome on the Cross Fells Trail but must be kept on a leash except in designated off-leash areas. See our [Dog Park page](/dog-park/) for off-leash zone details.

* * *

## Trailheads & Parking

The Cross Fells Trail can be accessed from multiple trailheads across the reservation. The most common starting points:

**East Side:** Access from the South Border Road area near Medford and Malden. Parking is available at the South Border Road lot.

**West Side:** Access near the Sheepfold area in Medford, which also connects to the dog park and several other trail systems. Parking is available at the Sheepfold lot off Elm Street.

For full parking details including lot locations, hours, and fees, visit our [Parking and Directions page](/parking/).

## What to Expect on the Trail

The Cross Fells Trail lives up to its name — it crosses the reservation rather than looping through one section. That means the terrain shifts as you go.

**Early miles (East side):** The trail starts with a mix of wooded paths and rocky outcroppings. The footing gets technical quickly, so watch your step.

**Middle section:** This is where the trail gets quieter. You'll pass through stretches of forest that feel more remote than you'd expect this close to the city. Keep an eye out for trail blazes — the blue markings can be easy to miss in sections with heavy tree cover.

**Western miles:** The trail opens up slightly as you approach the West Fells section. You'll have opportunities to connect with the [Skyline Trail](/skyline-trail/) and work your way toward Spot Pond or the Sheepfold parking area.

**Wildlife:** Middlesex Fells is home to white-tailed deer, red foxes, wild turkeys, and a variety of songbirds. Early morning is the best time to spot them.

## Connecting Trails

The Cross Fells Trail intersects with several other trails in the reservation, making it easy to customize your route:

-   **[Skyline Trail](/skyline-trail/)** — the Fells' signature ridge trail; connects for those looking to extend the hike
-   **[Rock Circuit Trail](/rock-circuit-trail/)** — a challenging loop through the East Fells; connects near the eastern trailhead
-   **[Mustang Loop Trail](/mustang-loop-trail/)** — a popular mountain biking loop; shares some trail corridor in the West Fells

## Tips Before You Go

-   **Wear trail shoes or boots.** The rocky terrain on the Cross Fells Trail is hard on road shoes.
-   **Bring more water than you think you need.** There are no water sources on the trail itself.
-   **Download an offline map.** Cell service can be spotty in the middle sections of the park. AllTrails has the Cross Fells Trail mapped and available for offline use.
-   **Start early on weekends.** Trailhead parking fills up fast on warm weekends, especially the Sheepfold lot.
-   **Check the season.** Spring trail conditions can be muddy and slippery. The trail is most enjoyable late spring through fall.
