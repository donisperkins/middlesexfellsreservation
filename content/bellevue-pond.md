# Bellevue Pond at Middlesex Fells Reservation

Bellevue Pond is one of the quieter spots in the West Fells. Smaller and less visited than [Spot Pond](/spot-pond/), it tends to attract the people who already know the reservation well — locals who want a peaceful walk around the water without the crowds that gather at the more popular trailheads.

If you're looking for somewhere to fish, sit by the water, or just take a slower walk through the West Fells, Bellevue Pond is worth adding to your route.

* * *

## Bellevue Pond at a Glance

-   **Location:** West Fells section, Middlesex Fells Reservation
-   **Type:** Freshwater pond
-   **Best for:** Fishing, nature walks, wildlife watching
-   **Swimming:** Not permitted
-   **Access:** Via West Fells trail network

* * *

## Getting There

Bellevue Pond is accessible from the West Fells trail network. The most common approach is from the **Sheepfold area** off Elm Street in Medford, which serves as the main hub for the West Fells. From there, follow the trails southwest into the West Fells toward the pond.

The walk in is part of the experience — the trails through the West Fells are quieter and more forested than some of the East Fells routes, and the approach to Bellevue Pond takes you through some of the more peaceful terrain in the reservation.

For parking details and directions to the West Fells trailheads, visit our [Parking and Directions page](/parking/).

* * *

## Fishing at Bellevue Pond

Bellevue Pond is a solid spot for freshwater fishing in the West Fells. Like the other ponds in the reservation, it holds a mix of species typical of Eastern Massachusetts freshwater — including largemouth bass and perch.

A valid Massachusetts freshwater fishing license is required for anyone 15 and older. Fishing from the shoreline is the standard approach here. For more on fishing throughout the reservation, visit our [Fishing page](/fishing/).

* * *

## Wildlife and Nature

The quieter setting at Bellevue Pond makes it a good spot for wildlife watching. The pond's wooded shoreline provides habitat for a variety of birds year-round — great blue herons, kingfishers, and various waterfowl are commonly seen. In warmer months, painted turtles bask on logs near the water's edge.

The surrounding West Fells forest adds to the experience. This part of the reservation sees less foot traffic than the East Fells, so early morning visits in particular can feel genuinely secluded.

## Nearby Ponds

Middlesex Fells has several ponds worth exploring across the reservation:

-   **[Spot Pond](/spot-pond/)** — the largest body of water in the Fells; kayaking, canoeing, and fishing
-   **[Wright's Pond](/wrights-pond/)** — another West Fells pond; smaller and tucked further into the forest
-   **[Middlesex Fells Reservoir](/middlesex-fells-reservoir/)** — a historic reservoir at the heart of the reservation

## Tips Before You Go

-   **Check trail conditions.** The West Fells trails to Bellevue Pond can be muddy after rain. Trail shoes are recommended year-round.
-   **Bring a fishing license.** Required for anyone 15 and older fishing in Massachusetts.
-   **No swimming.** Swimming is not permitted at Bellevue Pond or anywhere in the reservation.
-   **Dogs are welcome** on leash. The [Sheepfold Dog Park](/dog-park/) nearby offers off-leash time before or after your walk.
-   **Go early or on weekdays** for the most peaceful experience.
