# Horseback Riding at Middlesex Fells Reservation

Middlesex Fells Reservation is one of the few green spaces near Boston where you can still ride horses on designated trails. The reservation's bridle paths wind through wooded terrain in both the East and West Fells, offering riders a quieter, less-trafficked way to experience the park.

It's not a riding center — there are no rentals, lessons, or stables on site. But if you have your own horse and are looking for a trail ride close to the city, the Fells is worth knowing about.

* * *

## Horseback Riding at a Glance

-   **Horses allowed:** Yes, on designated bridle paths
-   **Rentals available:** No — riders must bring their own horses
-   **Trails:** Designated bridle paths throughout the reservation
-   **Best section:** West Fells — wider, more open trails better suited to horses
-   **Fees:** No additional fee beyond standard park access

* * *

## Where to Ride

Horseback riding at Middlesex Fells is permitted on **designated bridle paths only**. Horses are not allowed on all trails — some of the narrower, rockier singletrack trails in the East Fells are not suitable or permitted for equestrian use.

The **West Fells** section of the reservation has the most horse-friendly terrain — wider paths, more gradual grades, and less foot traffic than some of the popular East Fells routes. The bridle path network connects several trailheads and loops through forested areas away from the busier sections of the park.

Always check the [Trail Map](/trail-map/) before your visit to confirm current bridle path locations and any seasonal closures.

* * *

## Trailheads & Trailer Parking

Riders bringing horses to the Fells will need to plan ahead for parking. Standard parking lots are not designed for horse trailers, and not all lots can accommodate them.

**Recommended approach:**

-   Contact the DCR (Department of Conservation and Recreation) directly before your visit to confirm current trailer parking availability and any permit requirements
-   The West Fells trailheads generally offer the most practical access for equestrian visitors
-   Arrive early — on busy days, larger lots fill quickly even for standard vehicles

For general parking information, visit our [Parking and Directions page](/parking/).

* * *

## Rules for Horseback Riders

Middlesex Fells is a shared-use park, and riders should be aware of the rules that apply specifically to equestrian use:

-   **Designated trails only.** Horses must stay on bridle paths — riding on hiking-only or bike trails is not permitted.
-   **Yield appropriately.** Horses have the right of way on shared trails. Hikers and cyclists should step to the side and avoid sudden movements when horses pass.
-   **Pack out what you pack in.** Riders are responsible for their horses and any waste left on the trail.
-   **Follow seasonal closures.** Some trails close seasonally for maintenance or to allow ground recovery after wet conditions. Check with DCR for current conditions before heading out.
-   **No overnight stays.** The reservation does not permit overnight camping or horse boarding.

Full park rules are available on our [Rules & Regulations page](/rules-regulations/).

* * *

## Best Times to Ride

-   **Weekday mornings** are the quietest time on the bridle paths — fewer hikers, less bike traffic, and more relaxed conditions for horses that are sensitive to activity around them.
-   **Spring and fall** offer the best trail conditions. Summer can be hot on exposed sections, and winter trail conditions vary depending on snow and ice.
-   **Avoid riding immediately after heavy rain.** Wet bridle paths erode quickly under hoof traffic. Waiting 24–48 hours after significant rain helps protect the trails for everyone.
