# Pickerel Rock at Middlesex Fells Reservation

Pickerel Rock is one of those spots at Middlesex Fells that locals tend to keep to themselves. A dramatic granite outcropping in the East Fells, it rises above the surrounding forest and offers one of the better natural viewpoints in the reservation — with [Spot Pond](/spot-pond/) glinting through the trees and the wooded hillsides of the Fells spreading out around you.

It's not far from the main trailheads, but it feels tucked away enough to make the walk worth it.

* * *

## About Pickerel Rock

Pickerel Rock is a large exposed granite formation shaped by glacial activity thousands of years ago. Like much of the terrain at Middlesex Fells, it's a remnant of the same geological forces that left the reservation's characteristic rocky ridges, scattered boulders, and uneven forest floor.

The rock itself is big enough to scramble up and sit on — which is exactly what most people do. It's a natural stopping point, a lunch spot, a place to take a photo or just sit quietly and look out over the pond.

-   **Type:** Geological feature / trail destination
-   **Location:** East Fells section, Middlesex Fells Reservation
-   **Access:** Via the East Fells trail network
-   **Difficulty to reach:** Moderate — rocky trail approaches from multiple directions

* * *

## Getting to Pickerel Rock

Pickerel Rock is accessible from several trailheads in the East Fells. The most common approaches start from the **South Border Road** area, near the Bear Hill parking lot, or from the **Spot Pond** trailhead along Spot Pond Brook Reservation.

The trails leading to Pickerel Rock wind through the East Fells' characteristic terrain — rooted paths, granite ledges, and dense mixed forest. Footing is uneven, so trail shoes or boots are recommended.

From the rock, you're also well-positioned to connect onward to the [Skyline Trail](/skyline-trail/), the [Rock Circuit Trail](/rock-circuit-trail/), or the shores of [Spot Pond](/spot-pond/).

For parking locations and directions, visit our [Parking and Directions page](/parking/).

## What Makes It Worth the Hike

The view from Pickerel Rock is the main draw. From the top of the outcropping you get a natural, unobstructed look at Spot Pond below and the wooded hills beyond. It's the kind of view that reminds you how much open space is tucked away in this corner of Eastern Massachusetts.

Beyond the view, the rock itself is interesting — a textured granite surface that shows the marks of glacial movement if you know what to look for. Kids tend to love scrambling up it. Adults tend to linger longer than planned.

It's also a quieter spot than some of the more well-known destinations in the reservation. If you want to avoid the crowds that gather near the Sheepfold or the Bear Hill parking lot on weekends, the East Fells trails leading to Pickerel Rock are a good alternative.

## Connecting Trails

From Pickerel Rock you can extend your hike in several directions:

-   **[Skyline Trail](/skyline-trail/)** — the reservation's main ridgeline route; accessible from the East Fells trail network near Pickerel Rock
-   **[Rock Circuit Trail](/rock-circuit-trail/)** — a challenging loop through the rocky East Fells terrain; passes near Pickerel Rock
-   **[Spot Pond](/spot-pond/)** — a short walk from Pickerel Rock; great for a kayak launch or a break by the water (swimming is not permitted)
-   **[Trail Map](/trail-map/)** — full reservation map with all East Fells trails and Pickerel Rock marked

## Tips Before You Go

-   **Wear grippy shoes.** Getting to Pickerel Rock involves rocky, rooted trails. Trail shoes or boots are worth it.
-   **It's a great destination for kids** who like to explore and scramble — just keep an eye on the edges of the rock face.
-   **Pair it with Spot Pond.** Pickerel Rock and Spot Pond are close enough to combine into a single outing. Hike to the rock, then walk down to the pond for a break before heading back.
-   **Weekday mornings are ideal.** Like most East Fells destinations, Pickerel Rock is quietest early in the week and early in the morning.
-   **Dogs are welcome** on leash. The [Sheepfold Dog Park](/dog-park/) is a short drive away for off-leash time.
