# Swimming at Middlesex Fells Reservation

**Swimming is not permitted anywhere in the Middlesex Fells Reservation.** This includes all ponds and reservoirs — Spot Pond, Bellevue Pond, Wright's Pond, and the High, Middle, and Low Reservoirs in Winchester. The prohibition applies year-round and is enforced by the Massachusetts Department of Conservation and Recreation (DCR).

If you're visiting on a hot day and looking for water, see the options below.

* * *

## Water Activities That Are Permitted

While swimming is not allowed, there are several ways to enjoy the water at the Fells:

-   **Kayaking and canoeing** — Spot Pond is open for paddling through [Paddle Boston](https://paddleboston.com/rentals/our-locations/stoneham-spot-pond/) (rentals only; no personal watercraft). See our [Kayaking & Canoeing](/canoe-and-kayaking/) page for details.
-   **Fishing** — Spot Pond, Bellevue Pond, and Wright's Pond are open for fishing with a valid Massachusetts fishing license.
-   **Waterside walking** — The perimeter trails around Spot Pond and the reservoirs offer scenic water views and are a popular way to enjoy the ponds without entering the water.

* * *

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I swim at Spot Pond?** No. Swimming is prohibited at Spot Pond and all other water bodies within the Middlesex Fells Reservation.

**Is there a designated swim area anywhere in the Fells?** No. There are no designated swimming areas in the reservation.

**Where can I swim near the Fells?** The DCR operates several supervised swimming beaches and pools elsewhere in the greater Boston area. Visit the [DCR website](https://www.mass.gov/orgs/department-of-conservation-and-recreation) for locations and hours.
