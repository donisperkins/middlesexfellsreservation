# Mustang Loop Trail at Middlesex Fells Reservation

The Mustang Loop Trail is the go-to route for mountain bikers at Middlesex Fells. Located in the West Fells section of the reservation, it's a rolling, technically interesting loop that draws riders from all over the area — and has built up a loyal following among locals who come back to it week after week.

Hikers use it too, but if you're here on a weekend, expect to share the trail with bikes. It's a good one to know about regardless of how you get around the Fells.

* * *

## Trail Overview

-   **Difficulty:** Moderate
-   **Trail Type:** Loop
-   **Trail Markers:** Green blazes
-   **Section:** West Fells, Middlesex Fells Reservation
-   **Best for:** Mountain bikers, experienced hikers

The Mustang Loop winds through the West Fells with enough variety to keep things interesting — wooded stretches, rocky sections, and some technical features that make it a favorite for riders looking for a real workout without leaving the greater Boston area.

* * *

## Trailheads & Parking

The Mustang Loop Trail is most commonly accessed from the **Sheepfold parking area** off Elm Street in Medford, which is the main hub for the West Fells. It's also accessible from the Flynn Rink area and several pull-off points along the reservation boundary roads.

-   **Primary access:** Sheepfold lot, Elm Street, Medford, MA
-   **Lot type:** Free, paved
-   **Hours:** Open dawn to dusk
-   **Notes:** The Sheepfold lot is large but fills quickly on weekends. Arrive early or consider the alternate lots along South Border Road.

Full parking details at our [Parking and Directions page](/parking/).

## What to Expect on the Trail

The Mustang Loop earns its popularity by offering terrain that's genuinely fun to ride — and challenging enough to feel rewarding without being punishing. The trail flows well, with natural features built into the route rather than added on.

**For mountain bikers:** Expect a mix of flowy singletrack, rooty sections, and some rock moves that'll keep you engaged. It's not a black diamond trail, but it rewards riders who are comfortable off pavement and can pick a line. Hardtails and full suspension bikes both work well here.

**For hikers:** The Mustang Loop is a solid hiking option in the West Fells, especially if you want a quiet alternative to the more crowded routes. The wooded terrain keeps it cool in summer, and the loop format means you'll end up back where you started without retracing your steps.

**Trail conditions:** Like most trails at the Fells, the Mustang Loop can get muddy after rain. Consider waiting 24–48 hours after heavy rain before riding or hiking to protect the trail surface.

## Tips Before You Go

-   **Bikes must yield to hikers** on multi-use trails. Call out when passing and keep speeds reasonable on blind corners.
-   **Check trail conditions before you go.** The Fells trail network can take time to dry after rain. Riding wet trails damages the surface — give it a day or two after heavy rain.
-   **Bring a repair kit.** There are no bike shops or repair stations inside the reservation. A spare tube, pump, and multi-tool are worth carrying.
-   **Helmets are strongly recommended** for all riders, regardless of experience level.
-   **Dogs are welcome** on leash on the Mustang Loop. If your dog needs off-leash time, the [Sheepfold Dog Park](/dog-park/) is right at the trailhead.
