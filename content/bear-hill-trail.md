# Bear Hill Trail at Middlesex Fells Reservation

If you're looking for a short hike with a real payoff, Bear Hill is it. The trail to the summit is one of the more popular routes in the East Fells — and for good reason. At the top, you'll find the historic Bear Hill Observation Tower, open-air views of the Boston skyline, and easy connections to some of the best trails in the reservation.

It's also one of the most accessible starting points in the park. The Bear Hill parking lot off South Border Road is easy to find, well-marked, and puts you right at the trailhead.

* * *

## Trail Overview

-   **Difficulty:** Moderate
-   **Trail Type:** Out-and-back or loop (via connecting trails)
-   **Summit Elevation:** 317 feet — the highest point in Middlesex Fells Reservation
-   **Trail Markers:** Follow signs toward Bear Hill / Observation Tower from the Bear Hill parking lot
-   **Location:** East Fells section, Middlesex Fells Reservation

Bear Hill is the high point of the entire reservation. The trail gains elevation steadily from the parking lot, passing through rocky, forested terrain before opening up at the summit near the [Observation Tower](/observation-tower/).

* * *

## Trailheads & Parking

**Bear Hill Parking Lot** is the primary trailhead for this trail and one of the most commonly used lots in the East Fells. It's located off South Border Road and gives you direct access to Bear Hill, the Rock Circuit Trail, and the Skyline Trail.

-   **Address:** South Border Road, Stoneham, MA (look for the DCR signage)
-   **Lot type:** Free, unpaved
-   **Hours:** Open dawn to dusk
-   **Notes:** This lot fills up fast on weekends and sunny days. Arrive before 9am on busy days or plan to use an alternate lot.

For a full list of parking areas across the reservation, visit our [Parking and Directions page](/parking/).

## What to Expect on the Trail

The hike to Bear Hill summit is relatively short but earns its moderate rating with rocky footing and a steady climb. The trail winds through the East Fells' characteristic terrain — dense mixed forest, granite outcroppings, and the occasional open ledge where you can catch your breath and take in the surroundings.

**At the summit**, you'll find the Bear Hill Observation Tower — a historic stone structure that's been part of the Fells landscape for over a century. Climb the tower for elevated views of the Boston skyline to the south and the wooded hills of the reservation spreading out in every direction. It's one of the best viewpoints in the entire park.

From the summit, the [Skyline Trail](/skyline-trail/) continues west across the ridge, and the [Rock Circuit Trail](/rock-circuit-trail/) loops through the rocky terrain below. Many hikers use Bear Hill as a launching point for longer routes through the East and West Fells.

* * *

## Bear Hill Observation Tower

The stone tower at Bear Hill's summit — also known as **Elizur Wright Tower** — is one of the hidden gems of Middlesex Fells. Built in the early 1900s, it's a short climb up the interior stairs to an open platform with panoramic views.

On a clear day you can see:

-   The Boston city skyline to the south
-   The forested hills of the Fells stretching north and west
-   Spot Pond and Wrights Pond glinting through the trees

The tower is open and accessible during park hours. No admission fee. See our [Observation Tower page](/observation-tower/) for more details.

## Connecting Trails

Bear Hill sits at a natural crossroads in the East Fells. From the summit or nearby junctions, you can connect to:

-   **[Skyline Trail](/skyline-trail/)** — the Fells' signature ridgeline trail; continues west from Bear Hill toward the West Fells
-   **[Rock Circuit Trail](/rock-circuit-trail/)** — a challenging loop through the rocky terrain of the East Fells; starts near the Bear Hill parking lot
-   **[Cross Fells Trail](/cross-fells-trail/)** — connects the East and West Fells; accessible from junctions near Bear Hill
-   **[Trail Map](/trail-map/)** — full reservation map with all trail connections marked

## Tips Before You Go

-   **The parking lot fills early on weekends.** Bear Hill is one of the most popular spots in the reservation. If you're planning a weekend visit, aim to arrive before 9am.
-   **Wear grippy footwear.** The trail has plenty of exposed rock and loose gravel — trail shoes or boots are strongly recommended.
-   **Bring water.** There are no water sources at Bear Hill. Bring more than you think you'll need, especially in summer.
-   **The tower adds time.** Budget an extra 15–20 minutes if you plan to climb the tower and take in the views.
-   **Dogs are welcome** on the trail on leash. The [Sheepfold Dog Park](/dog-park/) is a short drive away if your dog wants off-leash time after the hike.
