# Parking at Middlesex Fells Reservation

Middlesex Fells Reservation has several parking lots spread across its 2,200 acres, serving hikers, mountain bikers, dog owners, and day visitors. This guide covers the main lots, their locations, and tips for busy weekends. For trail information, see our [Trail Map](/trail-map).

## South Border Road Parking Lot

The South Border Road parking lot is the most popular entry point to Middlesex Fells Reservation. Located along South Border Road in Medford, this lot provides direct access to the [Rock Circuit Trail](/rock-circuit-trail), [Skyline Trail](/skyline-trail), and miles of [hiking](/hiking) and [mountain biking](/mountain-biking) trails. It fills up quickly on weekend mornings — arrive before 9am to guarantee a spot.

## Upper Sheepfold Parking Lot

The Upper Sheepfold parking lot is the primary access point for the [Sheepfold dog park](/sheepfold-dog-park), one of the largest off-leash dog areas in Massachusetts. The lot is located off Pond Street in Stoneham and also connects to several hiking trails. On weekends this lot reaches capacity by mid-morning.

## Bear Hill Parking Lot

The Bear Hill parking lot offers access to the eastern section of the reservation including Bear Hill Pond and surrounding trails. It tends to be less crowded than South Border Road and is a good alternative when the main lots are full.

## Tips for Busy Weekends

Middlesex Fells parking lots fill fast on spring and summer weekends. A few tips:

-   Arrive before 9am, especially on sunny weekends
-   Check alternate lots before giving up — Bear Hill is often overlooked
-   Street parking is available along South Border Road when lots are full
-   Check [hours & fees](/hours) before visiting for seasonal access information

### Overflow Parking Options

When all main lots are full, limited street parking is available along South Border Road and nearby side streets. Always check posted signs for restrictions before parking on the street.

* * *

For directions, the reservation is accessible from I-93 and Route 28. The main visitor center and parking is at the Botume House, 4 Woodland Road, Stoneham, MA 02180.
