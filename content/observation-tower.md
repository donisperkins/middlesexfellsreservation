# Elizur Wright Tower (Bear Hill Observation Tower)

Elizur Wright Tower — also known as Bear Hill Tower — is a historic stone observation tower located at the summit of Bear Hill in Middlesex Fells Reservation. At 317 feet above sea level, it offers some of the best panoramic views in eastern Massachusetts, including clear-day sightlines to the Boston skyline.

## About the Tower

Built in 1910 and named after Elizur Wright, a 19th-century conservationist who helped preserve the Middlesex Fells, the tower is a four-story stone structure open to the public year-round. Climbing to the top is free and takes just a few minutes. The tower is one of the most distinctive landmarks in the reservation and a favorite destination for hikers.

## How to Get There

Bear Hill Tower is accessible by foot only — there is no road to the summit. The closest parking is at the [South Border Road parking lot](/parking) or the Bear Hill parking lot off Woodland Road in Stoneham. From either lot, the hike to the summit is under a mile.

## What You'll See From the Top

On a clear day the tower offers 360-degree views including:

-   The Boston skyline to the south
-   The Blue Hills Reservation to the southwest
-   The Middlesex Fells forest canopy in all directions
-   Spot Pond to the east

Sunrise and fall foliage visits are especially popular.

## Best Trails to the Summit

### Rock Circuit Trail to the Tower

The [Rock Circuit Trail](/rock-circuit-trail) passes near Bear Hill and is one of the most direct routes to the tower from the South Border Road parking lot. Expect rocky terrain and moderate elevation gain.

### Skyline Trail to the Tower

The [Skyline Trail](/skyline-trail) crosses Bear Hill summit and offers the most scenic approach to the tower. It's a longer route but rewards hikers with views along the entire ridge before reaching the top.
