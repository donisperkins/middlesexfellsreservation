# Rock Circuit Trail at Middlesex Fells Reservation

The Rock Circuit Trail is one of the most popular hiking trails at Middlesex Fells Reservation, winding through the rocky heart of the western section with rugged terrain, forest views, and access to the historic [Elizur Wright Tower](/observation-tower). It's a challenging but rewarding loop that showcases the best of the Fells' natural landscape.

## Trail Overview

The Rock Circuit Trail is a loop trail marked with yellow blazes that circles the rocky highland terrain of the western Middlesex Fells. It passes through dense forest, over granite outcrops, and near several of the reservation's scenic viewpoints. The trail is open to hikers and leashed dogs year-round.

## Distance, Elevation & Difficulty

-   **Distance:** 4.6 miles (loop)
-   **Elevation gain:** ~550 feet
-   **Difficulty:** Moderate to challenging
-   **Trail surface:** Rocky, rooted, uneven — hiking boots recommended
-   **Blazes:** Yellow

The trail is not recommended for young children or casual walkers due to the rocky and uneven terrain. Experienced hikers will find it a satisfying challenge.

## Trailheads & Parking

The main trailhead is accessible from the [South Border Road parking lot](/parking) in Medford, the most popular entry point for the western Fells. The Bear Hill parking lot off Woodland Road in Stoneham also provides access to the trail. See our full [parking guide](/parking) for directions and weekend tips.

## What to Expect on the Trail

The Rock Circuit Trail lives up to its name — expect a lot of rock. Much of the trail traverses exposed granite ledges and boulder fields, particularly in the central section near Bear Hill. The footing requires attention, especially when wet. The trail is well-blazed and easy to follow throughout.

Highlights along the route include views over the forest canopy from elevated ledges, connections to the [Skyline Trail](/skyline-trail), and the climb to Bear Hill summit.

### Rock Circuit Trail to Elizur Wright Tower

The Rock Circuit Trail provides one of the most direct routes to [Elizur Wright Tower](/observation-tower) at Bear Hill summit. From the South Border Road trailhead, the tower is approximately 1.5 miles in. It's a worthwhile detour — the views from the top on a clear day extend all the way to the Boston skyline.
