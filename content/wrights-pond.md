# Wright's Pond at Middlesex Fells Reservation

Wright's Pond sits tucked into the West Fells, one of the smaller and quieter bodies of water at Middlesex Fells Reservation. It doesn't get the attention that [Spot Pond](/spot-pond/) does, and that's part of what makes it worth visiting. If you want a peaceful walk by the water, a place to fish without competition for shoreline, or just a corner of the Fells that feels genuinely off the beaten path, Wright's Pond delivers.

* * *

## Wright's Pond at a Glance

-   **Location:** West Fells section, Middlesex Fells Reservation
-   **Type:** Freshwater pond
-   **Best for:** Fishing, quiet walks, wildlife watching
-   **Swimming:** Not permitted
-   **Access:** Via West Fells trail network

* * *

## Getting There

Wright's Pond is reached via the West Fells trail network. The **Sheepfold parking area** off Elm Street in Medford is the most practical starting point — from there, trails wind through the West Fells toward the pond.

The walk in is relaxed and forested, passing through some of the less-trafficked sections of the reservation. It's a good stretch of trail in its own right.

For parking locations and directions to the West Fells, visit our [Parking and Directions page](/parking/).

* * *

## Fishing at Wright's Pond

Wright's Pond is a small but fishable body of water in the West Fells. Like the other ponds in the reservation, it supports freshwater species typical of Eastern Massachusetts — including largemouth bass and perch.

A valid Massachusetts freshwater fishing license is required for anyone 15 and older. Shoreline fishing is the standard approach. For a full overview of fishing opportunities throughout the reservation, visit our [Fishing page](/fishing/).

* * *

## Wildlife and Nature

The wooded setting around Wright's Pond makes it a naturally good spot for wildlife watching. Great blue herons are regularly seen along the shoreline, and the quieter atmosphere compared to the East Fells means you're more likely to catch a glimpse of the reservation's resident wildlife — deer, foxes, and wild turkeys among them.

Early morning visits are best. The West Fells at first light is a different place than it is at noon on a summer weekend.

## Nearby Ponds

Wright's Pond is one of several ponds scattered across Middlesex Fells:

-   **[Spot Pond](/spot-pond/)** — the reservation's largest pond; kayaking, canoeing, and fishing in the East Fells
-   **[Bellevue Pond](/bellevue-pond/)** — another West Fells pond, similarly quiet and forested
-   **[Middlesex Fells Reservoir](/middlesex-fells-reservoir/)** — a historic reservoir at the heart of the reservation

* * *

## Tips Before You Go

-   **Trail shoes recommended.** The West Fells trails to Wright's Pond can be muddy after rain — especially in spring.
-   **Bring a fishing license.** Required for anyone 15 and older in Massachusetts.
-   **No swimming.** Swimming is not permitted at Wright's Pond or anywhere in the reservation.
-   **Dogs are welcome** on leash. The [Sheepfold Dog Park](/dog-park/) is nearby for off-leash time.
-   **Best on weekdays or early mornings** for the most peaceful experience.
