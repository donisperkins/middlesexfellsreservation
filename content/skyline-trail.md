# Skyline Trail at Middlesex Fells Reservation

The Skyline Trail is the longest and most scenic hiking trail at Middlesex Fells Reservation, tracing a rugged ridge through the western section of the park with panoramic views, rocky summits, and connections to some of the best terrain in eastern Massachusetts. It's the signature hike of the Fells — challenging, rewarding, and unmistakably New England.

## Trail Overview

The Skyline Trail is marked with white blazes and runs as a loop through the highest elevations of the western Middlesex Fells. It passes over multiple rocky summits including Bear Hill, where [Elizur Wright Tower](/observation-tower) offers 360-degree views on clear days. The trail connects to the [Rock Circuit Trail](/rock-circuit-trail) at several points, making it easy to extend or shorten your hike.

## Distance, Elevation & Difficulty

-   **Distance:** 6.7 miles (loop)
-   **Elevation gain:** ~800 feet
-   **Difficulty:** Challenging
-   **Trail surface:** Rocky ledge, rooted terrain, steep sections
-   **Blazes:** White

The Skyline Trail is best suited for experienced hikers. The rocky and uneven terrain requires sturdy footwear and careful footing, especially on wet days.

## Trailheads & Parking

The most common starting point is the [South Border Road parking lot](/parking) in Medford. The Bear Hill lot off Woodland Road in Stoneham also provides access. See our [parking guide](/parking) for lot locations and weekend arrival tips.

## What to Expect on the Trail

The Skyline Trail rewards hikers with some of the best views in the Boston area. The trail climbs repeatedly over rocky ridges before descending into forested valleys, making for a varied and engaging hike from start to finish.

Highlights include:

-   Summit views at Bear Hill and other high points along the ridge
-   The historic [Elizur Wright Tower](/observation-tower) at Bear Hill summit
-   Connections to the [Rock Circuit Trail](/rock-circuit-trail) for shorter loop options
-   Dense mixed forest with excellent fall foliage
