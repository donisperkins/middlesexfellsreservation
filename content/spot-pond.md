# Spot Pond at Middlesex Fells Reservation

Spot Pond is the largest and most scenic body of water in Middlesex Fells Reservation, spanning approximately 340 acres in Stoneham, MA. Just minutes from Boston, it's a popular destination for kayaking, canoeing, fishing, and wildlife watching — with stunning views of the surrounding forest year-round.

## About Spot Pond

Spot Pond sits on the eastern edge of Middlesex Fells Reservation and is bordered by wooded trails on three sides. The pond is part of the Middlesex Fells watershed and serves as a backdrop for some of the most scenic walks and paddles in the region. In fall, the foliage reflecting off the water makes it one of the most photographed spots in eastern Massachusetts.

## Kayaking & Canoeing

Spot Pond is one of the few places near Boston where you can kayak or canoe in a natural, forested setting. The pond is calm and well-suited for paddlers of all skill levels. Non-motorized watercraft only — no gas-powered boats are permitted.

## Fishing at Spot Pond

Spot Pond is stocked and offers good fishing for largemouth bass, yellow perch, and chain pickerel. A valid Massachusetts fishing license is required for anglers 15 and older. Bank fishing is available at several spots around the pond's edge.

## Paddle Boston Rentals at Spot Pond

Don't have your own kayak or canoe? [Paddle Boston](https://paddleboston.com/rentals/our-locations/stoneham-spot-pond/) operates a seasonal rental location at Spot Pond, offering kayaks, canoes, and stand-up paddleboards by the hour. Rentals are typically available late spring through early fall — check their site for current hours and pricing.

## How to Get There

Spot Pond is located off Spot Pond Brook Parkway in Stoneham, MA. The pond is accessible from several points around its perimeter.
